# 03. Operations, Talent & Certifications

**Confidential Document**  
**Prepared by:** Strategic Consulting Group  
**Subject:** Building the Delivery Engine

## 1. Talent Acquisition: The "Shadow" Model
Senior automotive engineers (CAPL/HIL experts) are expensive and rare to hire. You cannot build a high-margin business relying solely on seniors.

* **Hire for Aptitude, Train for Niche:** Hire bright recent graduates in Electronics, Mechatronics, or Computer Science.
* **The 30-Day Boot Camp:** Develop a rigorous internal training program covering:
  * CAN, CAN FD, LIN, Automotive Ethernet basics.
  * UDS (ISO 14229) fundamentals.
  * CAPL scripting and Vector toolchain navigation.
* **The Shadowing Phase:** Assign juniors to shadow a Senior Technical Lead on a billable project. The client pays for the Senior; the junior assists (writing basic CAPL scripts, documenting test cases) for free or at a massive discount, rapidly gaining real-world experience.

## 2. Global Delivery Hubs (Margin Engineering)
To compete on price while maintaining high margins:
* **Front-End (Onshore):** US, Germany, UK. Account Managers, System Architects, and Principal Engineers who interact daily with the client, gather requirements, and design the test architecture.
* **Back-End (Nearshore/Offshore):** Eastern Europe (Poland, Romania), India, or Mexico. This team executes the bulk of the CAPL script writing, ARXML parsing, and log analysis.

## 3. Required Certifications (The "Table Stakes")
To work directly with European OEMs and major Tier-1s, you must pass rigorous compliance checks.
* **ISO 9001:** Quality Management Systems. (Required immediately).
* **TISAX (Trusted Information Security Assessment Exchange):** Mandatory for working with the German automotive supply chain. Proves your IT infrastructure can handle confidential prototype data securely. (Start within first 6 months).
* **ASPICE Capability:** While you don't need formal certification on day one, your internal processes must map to ASPICE (Automotive SPICE) SWE.5 & SWE.6 engineering processes.

## 4. Hardware & Infrastructure Lean Start
* **Client-Furnished Equipment (CFE):** In the early stages, insist that the client provides VPN access to their remote HIL rigs, or physically ships the necessary Vector hardware (VN1640s) and ECUs to your lab. 
* Avoid buying $100k dSPACE rigs speculatively.
