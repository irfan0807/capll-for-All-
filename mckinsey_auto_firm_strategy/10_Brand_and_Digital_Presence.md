## 1. Branding Philosophy for a B2B Engineering Firm
In automotive consulting, **brand = technical credibility**. You are not selling lifestyle or emotion — you are selling competence, precision, and reliability to an engineer who will stake their project timeline on you.

**Core Brand Attributes:**
* **Specialist, not Generalist.** "We exclusively validate automotive embedded systems" is more powerful than "We are a technology consulting firm."
* **Engineer-led, not Sales-led.** Your most valuable brand asset is the quality of the technical content your engineers produce publicly — not a slick brochure.
* **Bias toward proof.** Quantified results ("40% reduction in test cycle time"), open-source tools, and public code repositories prove capability better than any marketing copy.

## 2. Website — Your Digital Headquarters

### Core Pages
1. **Home:** One headline that immediately states your niche. Example: *"Automotive ECU Validation. Automated. Accelerated."*
2. **Services:** Individual pages per service (HIL Automation, CAPL Development, UDS Validation, CI/CD Integration, ISO 26262 Support).
3. **Case Studies:** 3–5 anonymized, metric-rich project summaries. This is the most-read page by engineering decision-makers.
4. **Team:** Photos and bios for each engineer, including specific tool certifications (CANoe, dSPACE, Python) and notable project experience.
5. **Resources / Blog:** Technical articles published regularly (see Section 4). This drives organic search traffic.
6. **Contact:** Simple form, direct email address for technical inquiries, and a Calendly link for a 15-minute intro call.

### Technical SEO — The Keywords Your Clients Search
Focus your website content and meta descriptions on high-intent technical search terms:
* "CAPL test automation consultant"
* "CANoe vTestStudio integration services"
* "UDS ISO 14229 automated testing"
* "HIL dSPACE test engineer outsourcing"
* "functional safety ISO 26262 testing services"

## 3. LinkedIn — Your Primary B2B Client Channel

### Company Page
* Publish 3–4 posts per week. Mix between technical tips, project insights (anonymized), and industry commentary.
* Every post should either teach something specific or solve a problem. Never pure promotional content.

### Founder's Personal Profile (Most Important)
The founder's personal LinkedIn profile will generate more business than the company page in Year 1–2.

**Profile Optimization:**
* **Headline:** Not "CEO at [Company]." Instead: *"Automotive Validation Architect | CANoe • HIL Automation • UDS Diagnostics | Helping Tier-1s Automate Test Pipelines."*
* **Featured Section:** Pin your top 3 technical articles and a link to your GitHub.
* **About Section:** Write in first person. Tell the story of what problem you solve and for whom in the first 3 lines (before "read more" truncates).

**Weekly Content Schedule:**
| Day | Content Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Technical Tip | "One CAPL function pattern that eliminates 200 lines of repetitive UDS message code." |
| Wednesday | Industry Insight | "Why the shift to CAN FD is exposing critical gaps in legacy test harnesses." |
| Friday | Behind the Scenes | "How we set up a full CI/CD pipeline for an ADAS HIL rig in 3 days." |

## 4. Content Marketing — The Technical Authority Engine

### Whitepapers (Quarterly)
Write 4–8 page technical deep-dives on specific, painful topics that your ideal client is struggling with.

**High-Value Whitepaper Topics (Year 1):**
1. *"Automating UDS Diagnostics: From Manual CANoe Sessions to Python-Driven CI/CD Pipelines"*
2. *"ISO 26262 Test Coverage — A Pragmatic Guide for V&V Engineers Without a Safety Team"*
3. *"Beyond CANoe: Integrating vTestStudio into a Jenkins Automotive CI/CD Workflow"*
4. *"CAN FD to Automotive Ethernet: How to Migrate Your Test Infrastructure Without Scrapping Everything"*

**Distribution:** Gate these behind an email form on your website. Each download is a qualified lead.

### GitHub Repository (Always Public)
Publish open-source tools that demonstrate practical competency:
* A Python library that parses `.dbc` files and auto-generates CAPL signal access templates.
* A CAPL template library for UDS services (0x10, 0x22, 0x27, 0x2E, 0x14, 0x19).
* A Jenkins pipeline example for running CANoe headless in a CI environment.

**Why This Works:** An engineer searching Google for "CAPL automation script" finds your GitHub repo. They use your tool. They show it to their manager. Your name is now in their head when they have a budget conversation.

## 5. Conference & Event Presence

### Year 1 — Attend as an Expert Participant
Attend 2–3 targeted events. Do not exhibit (€8,000 booths are wasteful in Year 1).

**Recommended Events:**
* **Embedded World (Nuremberg):** The most important automotive embedded software event in Europe.
* **Vector Congress / CANoe User Meeting.**
* **SAE World Congress (Detroit)** if targeting US market.

**At the Event:**
* Submit a speaking abstract to present a technical case study (even a short 10-minute lightning talk).
* Set up 10–15 pre-arranged 30-minute 1:1 meetings with pre-identified targets using the event app.

### Year 2 — Host Your Own Micro-Event
Host a breakfast roundtable or evening dinner with 15–20 V&V Managers. A carefully curated, intimate technical discussion is more valuable than a trade show booth.
